Rescue Story ‚Äì First Common Cobra
Date & Location

March 19, 2016 ‚Äî Bhavnagar (nearby village)

The Rescue

It was a warm afternoon when I received a call from a nearby village on the outskirts of Bhavnagar. The villagers had spotted a cobra near one of the houses and were understandably frightened. This was my first-ever rescue of a Common Cobra, around 5 feet long.

When I reached the location, the cobra had taken shelter near the base of a wall, its hood slightly raised in defense. Maintaining calm was crucial; any sudden movement could have made the situation risky for both the snake and the people nearby. I carefully cleared the surrounding area, keeping the crowd at a safe distance, and used the rescue stick to gently guide the cobra into a safe container.

The rescue went smoothly. Once secured, the cobra was later released into a natural habitat away from human settlement. That day marked the beginning of my journey with snake rescues, teaching me respect, patience, and the delicate balance needed when working with wildlife.

Lesson: Respect and patience save lives ‚Äî for people and animals alike.

The Rescue

After making sure it was healthy and uninjured, I released it back into a safe natural area away from human settlements.
