The Compassion Movement

Annual Wildlife Rescue Campaign during Uttarayan (Kite Festival)

Since 2015, Harry Gosai has been at the forefront of Karuna Abhiyan ‚Äî a state-wide wildlife rescue mission organized during the kite festival of Uttarayan. Every year, thousands of birds, reptiles, and animals are injured by kite strings. Harry, along with The Wild Mirror team and the Forest Department, leads rescue, treatment, and rehabilitation efforts across Gujarat.

Karuna Abhiyan ‚Äî 2019

Karuna Abhiyan 2020

Focus on preventive measures alongside treatment.

Karuna Abhiyan ‚Äî 2020
Description

In 2020, the campaign continued with renewed strength and coordination between the Forest & Environment Department and various NGOs. Rescue operations were carried out actively during the Uttarayan period, from 10th to 20th January 2020.

Karuna Abhiyan 2021

Compassion and continuity during the pandemic.

Karuna Abhiyan ‚Äî 2021 (COVID-19)
Description

The 2021 edition of Karuna Abhiyan was carried out amidst the COVID-19 pandemic under strict safety protocols and limited mobility. Despite the challenges, compassion continued ‚Äî volunteers, rescuers, and veterinarians came together to ensure that no injured bird was left untreated.

  • Between January 10‚Äì20, 2021, a total of 1,468 birds and animals were treated under the campaign, with over 85% recovery success.
  • Safety SOPs included sanitisation, masks, minimal handling, and disinfection of all rescue tools.
  • Community awareness continued through online talks, posters, and local WhatsApp rescue groups.
Key theme: Compassion and continuity during crisis ‚Äî saving lives despite restrictions.

Karuna Abhiyan 2023

Major scale-up ‚Äî tens of thousands rescued.

Karuna Abhiyan ‚Äî 2023
Description

The 2023 edition of Karuna Abhiyan marked one of the most impactful years since its inception. With strong support from Forest Department, NGOs, and local volunteers, the campaign scaled to an all-time high in rescue numbers and awareness activities across Gujarat.

  • Over 13,000 birds were rescued statewide within a week, including endangered species such as kites, eagles, and owls. (DeshGujarat)
  • Dozens of mobile treatment vans and temporary rescue shelters were set up in Bhavnagar and nearby districts.
  • Volunteers and vets collaborated to ensure 24/7 response operations during peak festival days.
Key theme: Major scale-up ‚Äî record rescues, stronger coordination, and increased public participation.

Karuna Abhiyan ‚Äî 2024
Description

The 2024 Karuna Abhiyan represented a new benchmark in coordinated rescue and rehabilitation across Gujarat. With thousands of volunteers, veterinarians, and wildlife officers working round the clock, the campaign reached more communities than ever before.
